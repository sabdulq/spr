Title: COVID-19 Vaccination Progress Worldwide - A Case Study

Case Study Dashboard Code

Introduction:

In this case study, we explore a dataset named "customer_data" containing information related to COVID-19 vaccinations across different countries. The dataset includes various columns such as country, iso_code, date, total_vaccinations, people_vaccinated, people_fully_vaccinated, daily_vaccinations_raw, daily_vaccinations, total_vaccinations_per_hundred, people_vaccinated_per_hundred, people_fully_vaccinated_per_hundred, daily_vaccinations_per_million, and vaccines.


Data Exploration:

Before diving into the analysis and visualizations, we first examine the dataset's structure and content. The dataset provides a time-series record of COVID-19 vaccination data for various countries. It includes information on the total number of vaccinations, the number of people partially and fully vaccinated, daily vaccination rates, and vaccination rates per hundred people and per million people.


Data Analysis and Visualization:

To gain insights from the data, we performed SQL queries and created visualizations using Tableau. Let's summarize our key findings:


Total People Vaccinated Worldwide:

We used SQL to calculate the total number of people vaccinated in the world by summing up the "people_vaccinated" column from the dataset. Our analysis shows that a total of X million people have been vaccinated globally.


Vaccination Projection for Each Country:

Using SQL, we projected the number of people who will be vaccinated in each country by next year. This projection considered the current number of people vaccinated and added the daily vaccination rate for the next 365 days. It is important to note that this is a simple projection and actual vaccination rates may vary due to changing circumstances.


Total Vaccinations per Country:

We visualized the total number of vaccinations in each country using a bar chart in Tableau. The chart revealed that some countries have administered a significantly higher number of vaccinations compared to others.


Vaccination Rate per Hundred People:

Another visualization depicted the vaccination rate per hundred people for different countries. This chart showcased countries that have achieved higher vaccination rates per hundred people, indicating more widespread vaccination efforts.


Daily Vaccinations per Million People:

To understand the intensity of vaccination efforts, we created a bar chart to compare the daily vaccination rate per million people across countries. This chart highlighted countries with robust vaccination campaigns.


Conclusion:

Through this case study, we delved into the COVID-19 vaccination data and performed various analyses and visualizations to gain valuable insights. We discovered that X million people have been vaccinated worldwide. Additionally, our projection indicates the potential vaccination outcomes for each country by next year. Our visualizations allowed us to identify countries with higher total vaccinations and vaccination rates, indicating successful vaccination campaigns. It also shed light on countries with high daily vaccination rates per million people, showcasing their proactive efforts in combating the pandemic. It is important to note that this analysis is based on the data available at the time of the study and is subject to change as new data becomes available. The success of vaccination campaigns depends on various factors such as vaccine availability, government policies, public awareness, and response to new variants. Therefore, continuous monitoring and assessment are crucial for effectively managing vaccination efforts worldwide.In conclusion, the data analysis and visualizations presented in this case study provide a valuable snapshot of the COVID-19 vaccination progress globally. They offer important insights for policymakers, healthcare professionals, and researchers to gauge the effectiveness of vaccination strategies and guide future efforts to combat the pandemic successfully.

SELECT SUM(people_vaccinated) AS Total_people_vaccinated_in_the_world
                FROM newcvd.customer_data.cwd;
                SELECT 
                    country,
                    MAX(people_vaccinated) AS people_vaccinated_current,
                    MAX(people_vaccinated) + (365 * MAX(daily_vaccinations)) AS people_vaccinated_next_year
                FROM newcvd.customer_data.cwd
                GROUP BY country;
                CREATE TABLE customer_data.cwd_vaccination_projection1 AS
                SELECT 
                    country,
                    MAX(people_vaccinated) AS people_vaccinated_current,
                    MAX(people_vaccinated) + (365 * MAX(daily_vaccinations)) AS people_vaccinated_next_year
                FROM newcvd.customer_data.cwd
                GROUP BY country;
